Overgrowth (2020) was presented as my MFA thesis project at the University at Buffalo. A series of 20”x24” chromogenic prints, the work is concerned with an expanded view of the landscape beyond limited human perspective. Engaging with photographic process as a means to expand perception, these scenes at once transcend human egotism, while placing it as distinctly centered within our experience of nature. You can find the full text of the written thesis here: Overgrowth Written Thesis

Overgrowth was exhibited at CEPA Gallery in Buffalo, NY in the spring of 2021. Documentation above.
